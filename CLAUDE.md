# ChargeYYC Phase 2 Grant Application

## Project Information

**Program:** ChargeYYC Multi-Residential Pilot Program - Phase 2
**Project ID:** CY2857
**Application Period:** August 19, 2025 - November 18, 2025
**Building Type:** Multi-Unit Residential (Parkdale)

## Executive Summary

This application seeks funding through ChargeYYC Phase 2 to install EV charging infrastructure at our multi-residential property in Parkdale, Calgary. We successfully completed Phase 1 of the program, which included developing a comprehensive EV Charging Road Map. Based on that roadmap and contractor quotes, we are now ready to proceed with installation.

## Phase 1 Completion

We completed ChargeYYC Phase 1 and received approval for our EV Charging Road Map (Project CY2857). The roadmap assessed our building's electrical capacity, identified optimal locations for charging infrastructure, and provided a detailed implementation plan.

**Key findings from our Road Map:**
- Current electrical capacity and required upgrades identified
- Proposed number and locations of EV charging stalls
- Infrastructure requirements for Level 2 charging
- Implementation timeline and cost estimates

## Proposed Project Scope

Based on our approved Road Map and contractor estimate from Parkdale EV Infrastructure, we propose the following installation:

### Infrastructure Upgrades (EV Enabled Rebate)
- Electrical infrastructure installation from junction boxes back to service panels
- Installation of 208V/240V termination points for Level 2 charging
- Required electrical capacity upgrades to support EV charging loads
- Installation of EV Energy Management System (EVEMS) if required
- All necessary permits and engineering design services

### Charging Equipment (EV Charger Rebate)
- Installation of Level 2 (208V/240V) charging stations
- SAE J1772 standard plug heads
- Networked charging stations with cloud connectivity
- Professional installation by licensed electrical contractor

All proposed equipment meets program technical requirements:
- Canadian safety certification (cUL, ULC, cETL, CSA, or cQPS)
- New, purchased equipment (not leased or refurbished)
- Permanent installation
- Compliance with all local codes and fire safety regulations

### Innovative Feature: Vehicle-to-Building (V2B) Capability

Our proposed system will be designed to establish bidirectional connections between individual EVs and their associated unit electrical panels, enabling Vehicle-to-Building (V2B) and Vehicle-to-Home (V2H) capabilities where supported by the vehicle, charger, and electrical regulations.

**Climate Resiliency Benefits:**
- **Emergency backup power:** During power outages, compatible EVs can provide electricity to critical building infrastructure or individual units, enhancing community resilience.
- **Grid stability support:** V2B capability allows EVs to serve as distributed energy storage, potentially supporting Calgary's electrical grid during peak demand periods.
- **Future-ready infrastructure:** As V2B technology matures and more EV models support bidirectional charging, our infrastructure will already be positioned to take advantage of these capabilities.

**Innovation Leadership:**
- **Pioneering implementation:** Few multi-residential buildings in Calgary currently incorporate V2B readiness, making this project a leading example of forward-thinking infrastructure design.
- **Policy development opportunity:** Our implementation will provide valuable learnings for City policies regarding V2B in multi-residential buildings, including safety standards, metering considerations, and grid integration requirements.
- **Scalable resilience model:** Success with V2B capability will demonstrate how multi-residential buildings can contribute to community energy resilience while maintaining individual unit electrical metering.

This feature positions our project not just as an EV charging installation, but as a comprehensive energy resilience and grid integration solution that addresses both current EV charging needs and future energy system evolution.

## Funding Request

### EV Enabled Rebate
We request reimbursement of 50% of eligible costs for making parking stalls EV Enabled, up to $6,000 per stall (maximum $90,000 per property).

**Eligible costs include:**
- Engineering design services
- Electrical infrastructure installation (junction box/outlet back to service)
- Labour and construction costs
- Electrical and related permits
- Electrical service upgrade fees
- EVEMS equipment and installation (if required)

### EV Charger Rebate
We request reimbursement of 50% of purchase and installation costs for Level 2 charging stations, up to $1,000 per single-port station or $2,000 per dual-port station (maximum $10,000 per property).

**Eligible costs include:**
- Level 2 charging station purchase costs
- Licensed electrical contractor installation labour
- Associated conduit and materials
- Electrical permits
- Parking and electrical design for charging stations

## Technical Compliance

### EV Enabled Requirements
All parking stalls made EV Enabled will include:
- Termination point with 208V/240V junction box or 240V outlet, OR
- Newly-installed 120V outlet designated solely for EV charging

Adequate electrical capacity will be ensured through:
- Load calculation per Section 8 of Part 1 of Canadian Electrical Code
- Installation of approved EVEMS if load calculation determines service capacity is insufficient

### Charging Equipment Requirements
All charging stations will:
- Be Canadian safety certified (cUL, ULC, cETL, CSA, or cQPS)

- Be networked with cloud/server communication capability
- Remain networked for minimum two years
- Comply with all local codes, bylaws, and fire safety requirements

## Why Our Building is a Good Fit for ChargeYYC

Our building represents an ideal template for the ChargeYYC pilot program, with characteristics that are highly replicable across Calgary's existing multi-residential housing stock:

### Representative Building Archetype
- **4-storey wood frame construction (1999):** Our building represents the maximum height allowed under Calgary's building code at the time of construction. This archetype was the standard for multi-residential development in the 1990s, before code changes in 2014 permitted 5-6 storey wood frame buildings.
- **Part of Calgary's largest construction cohort:** The 1990s saw substantial growth in Calgary with approximately 75,000-80,000 structures built during that decade, making buildings from this era a significant portion of Calgary's housing stock.
- **Common construction method:** Wood frame with concrete foundation was the predominant construction method for mid-rise multi-residential buildings in this era, making our building typical of thousands of similar properties across Calgary.

### Individual Metering Advantage
- **Individual electrical meters and heating/hot water:** Each unit has separate utility metering, which is optimal for EV charging implementation. This configuration allows residents to pay for their own electricity usage rather than splitting costs across the building.
- **Energy conservation benefit:** Research shows that individually metered residences consume an average of 17% less energy, and submetering can reduce building energy consumption by 18-40%. This existing infrastructure makes EV charging cost allocation straightforward and fair.
- **Resident accountability:** Individual metering ensures EV owners pay for their own charging costs, removing a common barrier to EV infrastructure approval in multi-residential buildings.

### Strategic Location
- **Parkdale neighbourhood diversity:** Located in an established inner-city community that has evolved from primarily single-family homes (1940s-1950s) to include multi-residential properties, representing Calgary's urban intensification pattern.
- **Geographic diversity for pilot:** Contributing to program learnings from different areas of Calgary beyond newer suburban developments.

### Installation Complexity and Learning Opportunities
- **Typical 1990s electrical infrastructure:** Our building's 25-year-old electrical system presents the standard challenges faced by buildings from this construction era, including potential service capacity constraints and aging distribution panels.
- **Wood frame construction considerations:** Installing conduit and electrical infrastructure in wood frame buildings requires different approaches than concrete construction, presenting common challenges that many similar buildings will face.
- **Optimal parking layout:** Our single-level heated underground parkade is arranged in a horseshoe configuration around the electrical room, providing relatively short conduit runs from the service to parking stalls. This efficient layout is typical of 1990s 4-storey construction and will demonstrate cost-effective installation strategies for similar buildings.
- **Heated underground parkade advantages:** The enclosed, climate-controlled environment simplifies installation compared to outdoor surface parking and provides year-round EV charging reliability in Calgary's winter climate.
- **ENMAX coordination requirements:** Working with ENMAX to assess and potentially upgrade service capacity will demonstrate the utility coordination process for similar buildings across Calgary.
- **Balancing existing loads:** With individual unit heating and hot water already drawing significant electrical loads, we will demonstrate how to integrate EV charging through load management strategies (EVEMS) that other buildings can replicate.
- **Valuable troubleshooting documentation:** The installation challenges we encounter and solve will provide practical guidance for contractors and property managers tackling similar retrofits.

### Resident Demand and Community Support
- **Current EV ownership and demonstrated need:** One resident currently owns an EV and charges from the building's public electrical panel. This temporary solution demonstrates both existing demand and the limitations of our current infrastructure - the public panel cannot support the anticipated number of future EV chargers.
- **Strong near-term demand:** A resident survey indicates that approximately 10 EVs are anticipated in the near future, representing over one-third of our 28 units. This substantial demand demonstrates the urgent need for proper EV charging infrastructure.
- **Existing owner participation:** The current EV owner is willing to transition from the public panel to the new dedicated EV charging system, demonstrating resident commitment to a proper, scalable solution.
- **Demonstrated resident interest:** Completion of Phase 1 Road Map and passage of a general resolution at our November 4 Annual General Meeting indicate strong resident and board commitment to EV infrastructure and a forward-thinking approach to building improvements.
- **Barrier removal:** Currently, residents who own or want to purchase EVs face a significant barrier - lack of adequate home charging infrastructure. This project directly addresses this obstacle and enables the 10+ residents ready to transition to electric vehicles.
- **Future-proofing for new residents:** As EV adoption accelerates across Calgary, having charging infrastructure in place makes units more attractive to prospective buyers, protecting property values.
- **Community decision-making model:** As a condominium corporation, our project demonstrates how condo boards can achieve owner consensus and move forward with shared infrastructure investments that benefit all residents. Our AGM resolution shows successful community engagement and buy-in.
- **Enabling broader EV adoption:** Residents who currently can't own EVs due to charging limitations will be able to participate in the transition to clean transportation.

### Scalable Implementation Model
- **Template for similar buildings:** Success at our property will provide a replicable roadmap for the thousands of 4-storey wood frame multi-residential buildings constructed in Calgary during the 1990s-2000s.
- **Common electrical challenges:** The electrical infrastructure requirements and upgrade pathways we identify will be directly applicable to similar buildings from this construction era.
- **Documented learnings:** As part of the cohort, our experience will help inform City policies and provide practical guidance for other buildings with similar age, construction type, and metering configurations.
- **Cost benchmarking:** Our project will establish realistic cost expectations for similar buildings considering EV infrastructure, helping others plan and budget effectively.
- **Process documentation:** From board approval to ENMAX coordination to installation, we will demonstrate the complete implementation pathway for similar properties.
- **V2B innovation pathfinding:** By incorporating Vehicle-to-Building capability readiness, we will pioneer policies and best practices for bidirectional charging in multi-residential buildings, positioning Calgary as a leader in resilient, grid-integrated EV infrastructure.

### Community Climate Impact
- **Supporting Calgary's Climate Strategy:** Installing EV infrastructure will enable current and future residents to transition to electric vehicles, directly addressing the 33% of Calgary's GHG emissions that come from transportation.
- **Multi-residential climate equity:** 38% of Calgary's occupied dwellings are multi-residential. Without programs like ChargeYYC, residents in these buildings face significant barriers to EV adoption compared to single-family homeowners, creating transportation inequity.
- **Long-term infrastructure investment:** Preparing this 25-year-old building for the next generation of transportation demonstrates how existing housing stock can be retrofitted for climate action, showing that older buildings can be part of Calgary's climate solution.
- **Resident enablement:** Removing infrastructure barriers to EV adoption for residents who would otherwise be unable to charge at home, regardless of their environmental commitment or desire to reduce emissions.
- **Enhanced climate resilience:** Vehicle-to-Building capability adds a layer of energy resilience, allowing the building to better withstand climate-related power disruptions while supporting grid stability during extreme weather events.
- **Neighbourhood-level impact:** As one of the early EV-enabled buildings in Parkdale, we will contribute to normalizing electric vehicle adoption in established inner-city neighbourhoods, creating visible climate action in the community.
- **Multiplier effect:** By creating a successful template for 1990s-era buildings, this project can accelerate EV infrastructure deployment across thousands of similar properties, multiplying the climate impact far beyond our single building.
- **Grid modernization contribution:** V2B-ready infrastructure positions our building to participate in future smart grid initiatives, supporting Calgary's transition to a more resilient and flexible electrical system.
- **Intergenerational benefit:** Making sustainable transportation accessible to current residents while ensuring the building remains viable and attractive for future generations who will expect EV charging as standard infrastructure.

By participating in ChargeYYC Phase 2, we will demonstrate how a highly representative building archetype can successfully implement EV charging infrastructure, providing valuable lessons that can be scaled across Calgary's substantial 1990s-era multi-residential housing stock.
